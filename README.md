🚀 Continuing our Forge Learning Series, this week I would like to share a key concept that plays an important role in modern AI applications: **Vector Databases**.

📚 Traditional databases store structured information such as numbers, text, and records. However, AI systems need a way to understand and search information based on meaning rather than exact keywords. This is where **Vector Databases** come in.

🧠 A Vector Database stores data in the form of numerical representations called **embeddings (vectors)**. These embeddings capture the semantic meaning of text, documents, and other content.

🔍 When a user asks a question, the system converts the query into a vector and finds the most relevant information by measuring similarity between vectors.

### Why is this important for Forge?

✅ Enables semantic search across project and portfolio data.

✅ Helps retrieve relevant context for AI-generated responses.

✅ Improves the quality and accuracy of answers by providing meaningful information to the LLM.

✅ Supports Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) workflows discussed in the previous session.

💡 In simple terms, Vector Databases help AI systems find information based on meaning instead of exact word matching, making responses more relevant and context-aware.
